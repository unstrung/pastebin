# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors, maintainers, and community members pledge to make participation
in the Pastebin project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age,
body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse,
inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

The following behaviors are expected of all community members:

- **Be respectful.** Use welcoming and inclusive language. Treat others as you would
  want to be treated.
- **Be constructive.** Give and accept feedback gracefully. Focus on what is best for
  the project and the community, not on "winning" a disagreement.
- **Be collaborative.** Work together openly. Share knowledge, credit others' work,
  and ask for help when you need it.
- **Be patient.** Contributors come from all backgrounds and skill levels. Be patient
  with those who are learning.
- **Be transparent.** Communicate openly about your intentions, especially when
  proposing significant changes.
- **Assume good faith.** Interpret ambiguous messages charitably before assuming
  negative intent.

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable and will not be tolerated:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Offensive comments related to personal characteristics (see Our Pledge above)
- Deliberate misgendering or use of rejected names
- Sexualized language or imagery; unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information (doxxing) without explicit permission
- Deliberately disrupting discussions, issues, or pull requests
- Sustained disruptive behavior after being asked to stop
- Any other conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a
  professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, and Discussions
- Code reviews and commit messages
- The project's README, documentation, and wiki
- Any communication channel (Discord, Slack, email, social media) where a community
  member is representing the project

It also applies outside project spaces when an individual's behavior has a reasonable
likelihood of affecting the safety or well-being of community members.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.
They have the right and responsibility to:

- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions
  that violate this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor whose behavior is harmful, threatening,
  offensive, or otherwise unacceptable
- Communicate the reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face
temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other project leadership.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please
report it by contacting the project maintainer directly via
[GitHub Security Advisories](../../security/advisories/new) or by emailing the
maintainer privately.

All reports will be:

- Reviewed promptly and taken seriously
- Handled with discretion and confidentiality
- Investigated fairly, with all relevant parties given an opportunity to respond

The maintainer is obligated to respect the privacy and safety of the reporter. We will
not publicly disclose the identity of anyone who reports a concern without their
explicit consent.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences for violations:

### 1. Correction

**Trigger:** Use of inappropriate language or other mildly unprofessional behavior.

**Response:** A private written warning explaining the violation and why the behavior
was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

---

### 2. Warning

**Trigger:** A single significant violation or a pattern of minor violations.

**Response:** A formal warning with specified consequences for continued behavior.
No interaction with the people involved for a defined period. Violating these terms
may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

---

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Trigger:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained
inappropriate behavior.

**Response:** Temporary ban from all community interaction and public communication
for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is
allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Trigger:** Demonstrating a pattern of community standard violations, harassment of
an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of a class of people.

**Response:** A permanent ban from all forms of public community interaction.
